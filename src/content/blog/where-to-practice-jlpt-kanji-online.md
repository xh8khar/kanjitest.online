If you are studying Japanese and preparing for the JLPT, you have probably asked: **where can I practice JLPT kanji online for free?** With so many websites and apps available, finding the right resource can be overwhelming.

## What Makes a Great JLPT Kanji Practice Platform?

Before diving into specific resources, here is what to look for:

- **Level-specific content:** Practice materials organized by JLPT level (N5 to N1)
- **Multiple question formats:** Multiple choice, flashcards, and writing practice
- **Readings and meanings:** Both on'yomi and kun'yomi for each kanji
- **Context-rich examples:** Words and sentences, not isolated characters
- **Progress tracking:** The ability to track which kanji you have learned
- **Free access:** No paywalls or account requirements

## Other Free JLPT Kanji Practice Resources

### Jisho.org

**Best for:** Quick dictionary lookups and kanji radical search

Jisho is the most popular Japanese-English dictionary. While not a dedicated study platform, it is invaluable for looking up kanji readings, meanings, and example sentences. Its radical search is particularly useful when you encounter an unknown kanji.

### NHK News Easy

**Best for:** Reading practice with real Japanese news

NHK News Easy provides simplified news articles with furigana (reading annotations) for kanji. This is excellent practice for N3 and above learners who want to build reading comprehension with authentic content. The vocabulary and kanji used align well with JLPT topics.

### Anki (Free on Desktop)

**Best for:** Spaced repetition flashcards

Anki is a powerful flashcard system that uses spaced repetition to optimize memorization. Many shared decks are available with JLPT kanji sorted by level. The desktop version is free, and you can create custom decks or use pre-made ones that align with the JLPT kanji lists.

### Tae Kim's Grammar Guide

**Best for:** Understanding kanji within grammar patterns

Tae Kim's guide is a comprehensive Japanese grammar resource that also covers kanji readings within grammatical structures. It is particularly useful for understanding how kanji readings change in different grammatical contexts.

## How to Practice JLPT Kanji Effectively

Regardless of which platform you use, follow these principles for efficient kanji practice:

### 1. Start with Your Level

If you are a beginner, start with [N5 kanji](/n5/study/). Do not jump to higher levels until you have a solid foundation — each level builds on the previous one.

### 2. Use Multiple Modalities

Combine different practice methods for better retention:
- **Study pages** for detailed information about each kanji
- **Flashcards** for active recall and memorization
- **Vocabulary** for learning kanji in context
- **Practice tests** for exam simulation

### 3. Practice Daily, Even Briefly

Fifteen minutes of daily practice is more effective than two hours once a week. Use the [flashcard system](/n5/flashcards/) for quick daily review sessions.

### 4. Test Your Knowledge Regularly

Do not wait until you have "finished" learning kanji to test yourself. Take [practice quizzes](/n5/sets/1/) early and often to identify weak areas.
