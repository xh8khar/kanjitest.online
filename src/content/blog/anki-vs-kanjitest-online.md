If you are studying Japanese for the JLPT, you have likely heard of Anki — the powerful spaced repetition system (SRS) used by learners worldwide. But you may also have come across KanjiTest.Online, a dedicated kanji study platform designed specifically for JLPT preparation. Which tool should you use? The answer depends on your learning style, goals, and how much time you want to spend configuring your study system.

This comparison breaks down the strengths and weaknesses of both tools so you can make an informed decision.

| Feature | Anki | KanjiTest.Online |

## Detailed Comparison

### 1. Ease of Getting Started

**Anki** requires upfront configuration. You need to download the app, choose or create a deck, configure card templates, install add-ons, and understand the SRS algorithm settings. For non-technical users, this can be intimidating. Our [guide to using Anki for Japanese](/blog/how-to-use-anki-for-japanese/) walks through the process, but it still takes time.

**KanjiTest.Online** requires zero setup. Open your browser, choose your level ([N5](/n5/study/), [N4](/n4/study/), [N3](/n3/study/), [N2](/n2/study/), or [N1](/n1/study/)), and start studying immediately. All kanji data, readings, examples, and vocabulary are pre-loaded.

**Winner:** KanjiTest.Online — instant access with no learning curve.

### 2. Content Quality and Coverage

**Anki** shines in its flexibility. You can download decks created by experienced learners, like the JLPT Tango series or Kaishi 1.5. However, deck quality varies dramatically. Some decks have incorrect readings, missing examples, or outdated information.

**KanjiTest.Online** provides curated, consistent content for all JLPT levels. Every kanji includes:
- On'yomi and kun'yomi readings
- Multiple example words with sentences
- Cross-referenced vocabulary
- Grammar points for each level
- Radical breakdowns and mnemonics

The content is professionally organized and verified for accuracy.

**Winner:** KanjiTest.Online for consistency; Anki for breadth if you find good decks.

### 3. Spaced Repetition (SRS)

**Anki** is legendary for its SRS implementation, especially with the new FSRS algorithm. It tracks every card's history and schedules reviews at optimal intervals. For long-term retention of thousands of cards, Anki is unmatched.

**KanjiTest.Online** uses a different approach. Rather than a traditional SRS, it offers:
- Practice tests with randomized questions
- Flashcards for active recall
- Study pages for reference

For daily kanji practice and self-testing, this works well. However, if you want algorithm-optimized long-term scheduling, Anki has the edge.

**Winner:** Anki for pure SRS; KanjiTest.Online for immediate practice.

### 4. Vocabulary and Example Sentences

Both tools offer vocabulary learning, but in different ways. Anki relies on user-created or community cards, which may or may not include example sentences, audio, or accurate readings.

KanjiTest.Online includes vocabulary pages for every level:
- [N5 vocabulary](/n5/vocabulary/)
- [N4 vocabulary](/n4/vocabulary/)
- [N3 vocabulary](/n3/vocabulary/)
- [N2 vocabulary](/n2/vocabulary/)
- [N1 vocabulary](/n1/vocabulary/)

Each vocabulary entry includes the word in kanji, reading, English meaning, and example sentences. The vocabulary is drawn from JLPT-relevant materials.

**Winner:** KanjiTest.Online for ready-to-use vocabulary; Anki for custom vocabulary creation.

### 5. Study Flexibility

Anki's greatest strength is customization. You can:
- Create any card type imaginable
- Add images, audio, and custom CSS
- Include pitch accent notation
- Build complex card hierarchies
- Use add-ons for automated card creation

KanjiTest.Online is simpler but focused. You study kanji in the order they appear for your JLPT level, with consistent formatting and no configuration needed.

**Winner:** Anki for power users; KanjiTest.Online for learners who want to focus on studying, not configuring.

### 6. Practice Tests and Quizzing

Anki does not include built-in practice tests. You can create test-like card decks, but it is not the same as timed quizzes with multiple-choice questions.

KanjiTest.Online includes 20 practice sets per level with randomized questions. The [N5 tests](/n5/sets/1/) test recognition (kanji to reading and reading to kanji) with realistic JLPT-style questions. This makes it easy to gauge your progress.

**Winner:** KanjiTest.Online for test preparation.

## Who Should Use Which?

### Choose Anki if:

- You want algorithm-optimized spaced repetition for thousands of cards
- You enjoy customizing your study system
- You study multiple languages (not just Japanese)
- You plan to study Japanese for years and want a long-term SRS archive

### Choose KanjiTest.Online if:

- You want to start studying immediately
- You are preparing specifically for the JLPT
- You prefer structured, level-based content
- You want practice tests and quizzes alongside reference material
- You do not want to manage card decks and settings

## Use Both Together (Recommended)

The best approach is to use both tools in combination. Here is a proven workflow:

1. **KanjiTest.Online for daily study and practice tests** — Use the [study pages](/n5/study/) to learn new kanji and the [flashcards](/n5/flashcards/) for quick review.
2. **Anki for long-term SRS** — Create Anki cards from the kanji you studied on KanjiTest.Online, adding the readings and examples from the site.
3. **Practice tests for progress checks** — Take the [practice tests](/n5/sets/1/) weekly to measure your retention.

This hybrid approach gives you the structure and ready content of KanjiTest.Online combined with the powerful scheduling of Anki.

## Our Conclusion

Neither tool is strictly better — they serve different purposes. Anki is a powerful Swiss Army knife for spaced repetition across any subject. KanjiTest.Online is a specialized, polished tool for JLPT kanji study.

If you are a beginner who wants to start studying N5 kanji today without any setup, go with KanjiTest.Online. If you are an advanced user building a long-term SRS system for thousands of cards, Anki is essential. And for maximum effectiveness, use both.
